Affordable Housing in Portland, OR


To quote CNN's Kathryn Vasel: "Everyone wants to move to Portland."


Yes, Portland is hip. Portland is cool. And Portland is running out of space. As housing prices continue to rise, which neighborhoods are bearing the brunt?


Though the information in this map is broken down by census tract, you will find the neighborhood each census tract is a part of by mousing over the map.

Median Gross Rent

The first variable for your exploration is median gross rent. Gross rent is calculated by combining contract rent and estimated average monthly utilities. As a category, then, gross rent is meant to make rents comparable whether or not landlords include utilities in the contract price.


As explained in a 2016 Portland Housing Bureau study, cost of rent has risen faster in East Portland - a less wealthy part of the city known for being home to many of the city's East Asian immigrant families - than other parts of the city. Portland has seen a subsequent rise in no-cause evictions in areas with steeply rising rent.

Median Home Sale Price

Median home sale price is another indicator of housing affordability in Portland. A 2016 Oregon Office of Economic Analysis report shows that of 31 metro areas with comparable median household incomes to Portland, 28 had lower median housing prices.


From the maps presented here, it appears the sharpest increases in home sale prices were in North and East Portland.
